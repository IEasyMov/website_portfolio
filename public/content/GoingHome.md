<div id="introduction" style="display: flex; align-items: flex-start; gap: 2rem; margin: 2rem 0; max-width: 1200px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
    <div style="flex: 1; padding: 0 15px; color: #fff; text-align: justify; line-height: 1.6; max-width: 600px;">
        <h2 style="font-size: 2rem; color: #007bff; margin-bottom: 4rem;">🌟 Introduction</h2>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 4rem;">
            <i>Going Home</i> is a game where Sarah (The playable character), leaving work after a long week, has to return to her house... But will face an gigantic amount of obstacle on her way. Starting from the subway and metro not working, people asking for direction or help, even some dangers..?
        </p>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 0rem;">
            It's a narrative, point & click adventure game. The players slowly discover the universe and semi-futuristic city where Sarah lives, all the citizens and their secrets. events that are occuring in the city involving policy, industrial changements or even secret society.. 
        </p>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 3rem;">
            The players will play through different scenes representing streets, buildings, rooms, etc.. In which, are tons of characters and citizens. Each with their own path and story.
        </p>
        <p>I worked on this game during my Internship at Microïds Studio Paris (MSP), it was a new project of the studio, from scratch so everything was to imaginate and create.
            We were two working on it at full time, starting to create the universe and setup of the story thanks to the main ideas and vision of the Game Directors.
            Starting from brainstorming around temporal loops, then how the player was moving through the city, was the storyline linear or could the players explore a bit more than just the main story ?</p>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 4rem;">
            We answered all thoses questions during the 3 months we spent working on the project (3 months only because my Internship ended at that period) and it was very interesting and full of lessons to learn!
        </p>
        <p> 
            My Objectives for the project were simple but they were a lot :
        </p>
        <p>- Creating an nice universe (As original and weird as possible, but fitting to our visions and ideas)</p>
        <p>- Imagining charismatic citizens for the city</p>
        <p>- Setup a prototype to "sell" the idea to the Game Directors</p>
        <p>- Conceptionate the dialogue and interactive system (That one was really hard)</p>
        <p>- Make a game simple but deep, so the players loosing themselves in the story would understand it nicely and want to know more about the lore</p>
